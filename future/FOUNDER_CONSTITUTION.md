# EulerMind Founder Constitution (v1 — PARKED until after ADTC submission)

**Standing rule:** per `DO_NOT_BUILD.md`, deferred ideas live here as
notes, never as code on main. Nothing in this document authorizes any
pre-submission work. It exists to END the strategy loop, not to feed it.
Locked from the converged positions of the 2026-07-04 strategy sessions;
revisit only after the Devpost submit button is pressed.

## 1. Identity
EulerMind exists to **give every learner honest intelligence — answers
that know whether they've been proved — regardless of connectivity.**

## 2. First product (the wedge)
One product: the **offline maths companion** a West African secondary
student opens voluntarily, alone, the night before an exam. Solves
step-by-step, checks its own work deterministically, and labels every
answer with earned trust only. Not a platform. Not an OS. Not an
ecosystem.

## 3. Customer vs user
Daily user: **the student** (their voluntary second re-open decides
everything). Paying customers, ranked by tractability: private-school
proprietors → parents (already spending on "lesson" and past questions)
→ tutorial centres → NGOs/donors → government last (after proof, never
first). Schools don't buy pedagogy; proprietors buy pass-rate
perception and cost reduction.

## 4. Mission vs execution
20-year mission (fundraising sentence): trusted learning infrastructure
for low-connectivity education. Operating sentence (the only one that
touches decisions): *the offline maths companion a student trusts at
11pm.* The mission never grants scope permission.

## 5. Moats, ranked by defensibility
1. **Calibrated honesty as brand** — the AI that has never lied to a
   child about maths, or about the child. Incumbents can't copy it
   without breaking their always-confident consumer products.
2. **Offline → private → trusted-with-memory** — the learner's record
   never leaves their control; cloud-native rivals structurally can't
   match it.
3. **Curriculum-anchored longitudinal learner model** — mastery
   evidence welded to a validated topic map; compounds per student,
   creates switching cost (the portable learner file). Either half
   alone is weak; welded, defensible.
4. Distribution into low-connectivity schools (logistics + trust
   relationships, not code).
5. Technology last — the model is a commodity underneath.

## 6. Flywheel
Student uses it alone → honest labels earn trust → student returns →
local evidence accumulates → adaptation improves and the learner file
gains value → results and word-of-mouth inside the school → proprietor
adopts lab-wide → more students → (k-anonymous gap aggregation, human-
approved) → next version targets measured need → more trust.

## 7. Principles (engineering law)
1. Never fabricate certainty — every label is earned by running code.
2. Offline is sacred; the core never requires the cloud.
3. Students own their learning history — local, portable, private.
4. Store evidence, not conclusions.
5. Every claim traces to a measurement.
6. Verify wherever checkable; checking is cheaper than generation.
7. Calibration applies to ourselves — mastery estimates, privacy
   claims, and marketing copy are held to the same bar as certificates.
8. Depth before breadth: one subject done exceptionally.
9. Automation prioritizes; humans approve curriculum. Always.
10. The metric is the voluntary second re-open — trust measured by
    return, never by demo.

## 8. What we refuse to build
General chatbot · engagement-addiction mechanics · cloud dependency in
the learning core · fake confidence ("just make it answer everything")
· exam-answer leak farms · autonomous curriculum invention · sale of
student data, ever · OS-first architecture · any feature that cannot
state its evidence.

## 9. Horizons (nothing may appear early)
- **H1 — ADTC (now):** frozen submission + Σ1 completion only.
- **H2 — first schools (first 6 months post-ADTC):** one lab, one exam
  cohort; learner passport; read-only teacher view; the only deliverable
  is retention evidence and a calibration test of the mastery model.
- **H3 — national:** more subjects/exam boards; k-anonymous evolution
  engine; school groups; first ministry conversations.
- **H4 — generational:** the trusted learning layer for low-connectivity
  education; phones; anglophone Africa and beyond.

## 10. The killer insight
When the giants ship free offline educational models, EulerMind still
exists because they ship **answer engines optimized to sound sure**, and
we ship a **companion whose product is knowing what it proved** — plus
the student's private longitudinal record, which lives with the student
and not on anyone's server. "Our model is better" is never the answer;
if it ever becomes the answer, we have failed.

## 11. The one sentence
**EulerMind is the offline maths companion that knows the difference
between what it has proved and what it has only inferred — and never
pretends otherwise.**
